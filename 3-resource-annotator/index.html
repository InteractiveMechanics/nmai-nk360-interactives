NORTHERN PLAINS HISTORY & CULTURE: CROW NATION

Privileges & Responsibilities

In this passage from The Sixth Grandfather: Black Elk's Teaching Given to John G. Neihardt, Black Elk (Oglala Lakota) recounts a story that involves how, with the use of a pipe and a sacred bundle, the Crow and Arapahoe Nations established peace.

Annotate this material

The late Joseph Medicine Crow was his tribe's historian of distinction. Mr. Medicine Crow performe the four deeds necessary to become a Crow Chief during World Wat II: He captured a well-guarded horse; took an enemy's weapon; counted coup on (struck) the enemy; and led a successful war party and returned home safely.

Annotate this material

Secretary Salazar of the U.S. Department of the Interior joined Crow Tribe (Apsáalooke Nation) Chairman Cedric Black Eagle and Montana Governor Brian Schweitzer for the signing of a Crow Tribe-Montana Water Rights Compact. The compact seeks to resolve more than three decades of controversy, ensure safe drinking water for the resercation, and provide for the regabilitation of the Crow Irrigation Project.

Original Text

"Sharp Nose, an Arapahoe citizen, made a sacred bundle and with his pipe in hand approached a Crow community, not knowing whether he or his brother-in-law Big Plume would be spared. After all, the two Arapahoe's intent was to retrieve horses some Crows had taken from them.
However, these two nations understood the bundle's import, which included a pipe.
